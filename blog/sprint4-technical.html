Technical Blog 4 - Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Understanding that the brain literally creates new and stronger connections when you perform activities. This indicates that anyone can learn anything. This can be used to counteract ideas such as “being bad at math because I’m not a math person”, enabling that individual to achieve greater joy and achievement in their life.

Discuss how might engage with the principals of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

Because new and stronger connections are created, I’ll always be able to perform better if I just persist and practice, no matter how bad I believe myself to be at doing a particular task.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity

Sleep: your brain creates new connections while sleeping and filters out all the toxins it produces.

Meditation: Studies have shown that this increases the rate of neuroplasticity

Exercise: Aerobic exercise and high intensity exercise in particular, increases the production of BDNF (brain derived neurotrophic factor) which creates new brain cells, increases synapses and preserves brain cells.

Curcumin: This spice reduces brain inflammation, preserves healthy brain cells and increases BDNF

It’s the mindset that you can learn anything. This means that there is nothing you can not learn and that all the activities that you have put off because you told yourself “I’m not a art/math/programmer” person, are not valid reasons and that you can achieve those skills

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I won’t tell myself I can’t learn anything or if I encounter something that’s difficult, I will understand that it will make me growth and is a useful learning experience. And if I catch myself doing so, I’ll ask why am I unable to learn it.
