Technical Blog 4 - Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

For my last assignment I was initially quite stumped on how to remove all "buzz" words from a bunch of text. I was stumped on how I should go about the task and initially didn’t know where to begin.

However after taking a small break, and asking myself what were the problems where and what I had to do, the solution became quite clear. In the end, I managed to elegantly complete the task with only 4 lines of actual code.

From this I learnt that asking yourself and explaining to yourself “what exactly am I trying to do in terms of subtasks” is really important and can break a complex problem into manageable, small tasks.

Reflect on how confident you feel using the problem solving techniques and process:

Pseudocode: I believe I’m not the best at pseudocode as most of the time I like to do planning in my head instead. However for more complex tasks, I tend to use pseudocode as it helps me manage more thoughts.

Trying something: I quite often just test stuff out in the chrome developer console and thus I believe I’m quite experience at this.

Rubber ducky method: I believe this could skill could be developed more. I usually use other problem solving techniques so I believe it does not pose much of an issue.

Reading error messages: This is another area which I believe I could improve on, as I find myself hesitating to look at the errors on occasion.

Console.logging: This is my main problem solving technique and I believe I’m quite good at it.

Googling: I often learn stuff on my own and have been googling for information for a very long so I believe I’m quite proficient at this.

Asking your peers for help: This is likely an area which I can improve on. I tend not to use this method of problem solving as it is unreliable and hugely dependent on external factors

Asking coaches for help: This is an area which I can improve on. I tend not to use this method of problem solving often as they won’t always be available and thus I should learn how to solve problems through more permanent methods.

Improving your process with reflection: I believe I reflect relatively often but there is still room for improvement as I only reflect occasionally.
